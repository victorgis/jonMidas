UI/UX Design, UX Research





The OurPass Mobile app had a simple goal (well not so simple when you really think about it), help shoppers checkout!!!. Simple right? I guess so, unfortunately though being simple and being done happen to be two extremes.

Currently, Nigeria is one of the biggest markets when it comes to fintech products in Africa. A quick look at most fintech products in Nigeria, and you’ll be sure to find the classic send and receive money features, upon which other features are then built upon.

In keeping up with this tradition, the goal of the OurPass mobile app was to help online shoppers buy and pay for products online with as much ease as possible, by giving them access to platforms that had integrated the OurPass Checkout button on the app. This coupled with the classic send and receive money feature, would help give the app more use cases.

This meant that emphasis would be placed on the checkout experience, while the send and receive money feature will serve as an addendum.

What we had in Mind

What we came up with



I joined the team as part of efforts to help improve the overall experience of its available products offerings in the market. We however at some point had to rethink the entire experience of the mobile app, especially as regards the checkout experience.

After weeks of gathering user feedback, our fears became justified: Most users merely saw the mobile app as another fintech app for sending, receiving money, and paying bills, and this was totally not what we had initially intended. At this point, our mission became redefined: (Your Mission Should You Choose to Accept is to) Redesign the OurPass Mobile App.



We began by creating a list of items on the existing app that were supposed to serve as addendums, we separated these from those items that were an integral part of the checkout process, this helped us decide what we would prioritize or de-prioritize.

One of the goals of the redesign was simplicity. The existing app had some redundant elements and sometimes involved more steps than necessary to carry out simple tasks, we aimed to change this also.

Hence, the goal of the redesign became clear:

1. Design the OurPass Mobile app to help online shoppers Checkout in one click!!!
2. Reduce users efforts, by reducing number of clicks needed for each action
3. Design a more pleasurable interface.



The entire goal of the redesign was to create an experience that’d help users checkout on the OurPass mobile app in one click, hence our redesign had to go through vigorous checks at various times; these checks were done mostly through remote unmoderated user tests, and this helped us gain massive insight as regards users perception of our designs.

Below are some of the redesigns and how they looked in comparison to the previous



Home page

Version1

Redesign

The home page experienced a massive design overhaul. We saw it evolve from the regular send and money experience to a more checkout focused experience.

do this, we figured we would need to bring to the fore all features of the products that were directly related to the checkout experience. The previous design treated explore store as a by product in the platform, but we knew if we were going to sell the idea of the one-click checkout experience, we would need to help users find stores that had integrated the ourpass one click checkout experience into their platform, and the explore stores section would be super important for this, hence we decided to create a section on the home page for it, in addition to the fact thst it already had a place on the menu, this was the sort of importance we placed on it.



Checkout Analytics

Version1

Redesign

In keeping up with the goal of creating a more checkout centred experience on the mobile app, we had to change the parameters we used for analytics. Analytics on the previous design was more transaction focused, we aimed to change this.

After much discussions with users and other stakeholders, we knew analytics had to focus more on checkout experience rather than being just transactionary based. This meant analytics would measure number of products bought, number and value of orders, stores patronized etc.
